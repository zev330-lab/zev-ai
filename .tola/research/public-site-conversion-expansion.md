# Chokhmah Expansion Document: Public Site Conversion & Lead Generation

**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Scope:** Evaluating askzev.ai against best-practice AI consulting websites for conversion, trust, and lead generation. Identifying gaps, opportunities, and the full universe of what a showpiece consulting site could include.

---

## 1. Competitive Landscape & Existing Solutions

### What Top AI Consulting Sites Do

**Enterprise firms** (McKinsey, PwC, Accenture, Infosys):
- Lead with _outcomes and industries_, not technology. Headlines are about "unlocking value" and "responsible AI transformation."
- Massive case study libraries organized by industry vertical.
- Video testimonials from named C-suite clients with logos.
- Gated content (whitepapers, reports) as primary lead capture — email-for-content exchange.
- Calendly/HubSpot calendar embeds for "book a briefing" flows.
- Trust signals everywhere: partner logos (AWS, Google, Microsoft), certification badges, analyst rankings (Gartner, Forrester).

**Boutique/specialist firms** (Xcelacore, Cieden, RTS Labs, Stack):
- Position around _speed, focus, and personalization_ vs. big-firm bureaucracy.
- Feature named consultants with bios, LinkedIn profiles, headshots.
- Smaller but specific case studies with measurable ROI metrics.
- Interactive tools (AI readiness assessments, ROI calculators) as lead magnets.
- Blog content positioned as thought leadership to capture organic search.
- "Book a free consultation" as primary CTA (lower friction than a 12-step form).
- FAQ pages answering "how much does AI consulting cost?" and "how long does implementation take?"

**Solo practitioners / boutique AI builders:**
- Most competitive advantage comes from _niche specialization_ and _visible personal brand_.
- AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity) weight thought leadership and specialist positioning more heavily than generalist content.
- The strongest solo sites show the practitioner's face, name their clients by name, and include video walkthroughs of actual systems they built.
- Pricing transparency is a differentiator — most big firms hide pricing entirely.

### Where Zev.AI Currently Sits

The site is architecturally sophisticated (TOLA framework, 11 agents, full assessment pipeline), visually polished (dark navy theme, Framer Motion, serif typography), and has good foundational content. It sits in a unique position: the _technology underneath_ is genuinely more impressive than most boutique competitors, but the public site undersells this advantage in several critical ways documented below.

## 2. Conversion Funnel Analysis

### Current Funnel: Homepage -> Services -> Discover (12-step form)

**Strengths:**
- The hero headline ("AI systems that drive revenue") is outcome-forward and direct.
- The "Problem" section (four pain points) is well-written agitation copy.
- The 4-phase journey (Assess, Build, Optimize, Scale) provides clear service structure.
- Pricing transparency ("Starting from $2,500") is a major differentiator vs. firms that hide pricing.
- The discovery form is beautifully designed — step-by-step, Typeform-style, with progress bar.
- The chat widget (auto-qualifying leads after 3+ messages) is a smart conversion mechanism.

**Weaknesses / Gaps:**

1. **No headshot, no video, no human face anywhere on the public site.** This is the single biggest conversion killer for a solo consultancy. Research shows video testimonials increase conversion by 80%. The about page has text but no photo of Zev. At this price point, prospects need to trust a person, not a brand.

2. **Zero client testimonials or quotes.** The case studies describe results but include no named client quotes, no "here's what the CEO said" pull quotes, no short endorsement snippets near CTAs. The case study companies (ButcherBox, Blank Industries, Rosen Media Group, etc.) have metrics but no human voice. Even one sentence from a real person per case study would transform credibility.

3. **Only one conversion path.** Every CTA on the site leads to the same 12-step discovery form. This is a high-commitment action. There is no low-friction entry point for someone who is interested but not ready to commit 5 minutes. No newsletter signup, no downloadable resource, no "book a 15-minute call" calendar embed, no email capture in exchange for an AI readiness assessment.

4. **The discovery form has 12 required steps before submission.** This is appropriate for qualified, high-intent leads but filters out everyone else. B2B conversion benchmarks show form length negatively correlates with completion rate. The form has no "save and continue later" mechanism and no indication of what you get AFTER submitting (the AI pipeline running behind the scenes is invisible to the prospect).

5. **No urgency or scarcity signals.** No "currently accepting 3 new clients" or "next availability: April" or "limited assessment slots." Solo consultants' most powerful conversion lever is scarcity of their own time — the site doesn't use it.

6. **The blog exists but doesn't serve the funnel.** Blog posts are generated by the content pipeline, but there is no email capture on blog posts, no lead magnet offered in blog content, no "related services" sidebar linking blog readers to the discovery form, and no category structure that maps to buyer stages.

7. **"Our approach" is the secondary CTA on the homepage hero.** This sends interested prospects to a deep technical page about coordination patterns and sacred geometry. A prospect considering AI consulting does not need to understand hub-and-spoke routing — they need to see results and trust the person. The secondary CTA should be "See our results" (linking to /work) or "Book a call."

8. **The site says "we" but Zev is a solo practitioner.** This creates a credibility mismatch — visitors will discover it's one person and may feel misled. Boutique positioning research shows solo practitioners convert better when they lean into being one person ("I" language), not when they fabricate a team ("we" language). The strongest solo consultant sites own the personal brand.

9. **No pricing page or pricing FAQ.** Pricing is embedded in the services page, but there is no dedicated pricing page that SEO can index. "How much does AI consulting cost?" is a top search query in this space. A standalone pricing or FAQ page would capture significant organic traffic.

10. **No social proof near CTAs.** Research shows social proof should appear immediately adjacent to call-to-action buttons and forms — the moment of highest doubt. The CTA sections on every page are plain text + button with no testimonial, logo bar, or metric reinforcement.

## 3. Content & Feature Universe

### What a Complete AI Consulting Website Could Include

**Trust & Credibility Layer (currently missing entirely):**
- Client logo bar / "Trusted by" strip (even 3-4 logos transform perceived credibility)
- Named testimonial quotes (short, outcome-focused, with name/title/company)
- Video testimonial or video case study walkthrough (even one 60-second video)
- Media mentions / "As featured in" strip (if applicable)
- Professional headshot of Zev (ideally in context — at a desk, with screens showing dashboards)
- LinkedIn profile link prominently displayed (validates the person exists and has a network)
- "X years of experience" or "X projects delivered" concrete credentials

**Low-Friction Conversion Paths (currently only the 12-step form):**
- "Book a 15-minute call" with Calendly/Cal.com embed (highest-converting CTA for consulting)
- Newsletter / email list signup (blog sidebar, footer, or exit-intent popup)
- AI Readiness Assessment (interactive quiz, 5 questions, gives a score + email capture)
- Downloadable resource (e.g., "AI Implementation Checklist for SMBs" PDF in exchange for email)
- ROI Calculator (interactive tool: input your team size, hours spent on manual tasks, estimated savings)
- "Get a free mini-audit" — submit your website URL and get a 2-minute AI-generated assessment

**Middle-of-Funnel Content (currently absent):**
- Webinar recordings or live workshops
- "AI Implementation Guide" gated content
- Comparison pages ("AI Consulting vs. Hiring In-House" — captures organic search)
- Industry-specific landing pages ("/ai-consulting-for-ecommerce", "/ai-consulting-for-media")
- Cost breakdown page ("What does an AI system actually cost to build and run?")

**FAQ / Objection Handling (currently absent):**
- Dedicated FAQ page or FAQ sections on key pages
- Common questions: "How long does implementation take?", "Do I need a technical team?", "What if the AI makes mistakes?", "How do you handle data security?", "What happens after the project ends?"
- These questions are what AI search engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT search) pull from to recommend consultants

**Presentation / Demo Page (specific request):**
- A dedicated "/demo" or "/capabilities" page Zev can walk a prospect through on a call
- Could include: live dashboard preview (screenshot or embedded read-only view of the admin), architecture diagram, case study highlights in a linear narrative, interactive before/after slider showing manual process vs. automated, and a "here's what your system could look like" section
- The admin TOLA dashboard IS the demo — the question is how to surface a taste of it publicly

**Legal & Professional Pages (some missing):**
- Privacy Policy (required for any site collecting data)
- Terms of Service
- Cookie consent banner (if using analytics)

**Analytics & Tracking (unknown current state):**
- Google Analytics 4 or Plausible/Fathom
- Conversion event tracking on form submissions
- Heatmap tool (Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity) to see where visitors drop off
- UTM parameter tracking for inbound links

## 4. Product Completeness Analysis

### What Users See BEFORE They Sign Up

Currently: Hero -> Problem Section -> Journey Overview -> Case Study Summaries -> Principles -> CTA

**Missing before the conversion decision:**
- No face of the practitioner
- No voice of a client
- No concrete "here is what you receive" deliverable preview (what does an AI Opportunity Roadmap actually look like?)
- No sample output or preview of work product
- No "here's what happens after you submit the form" explanation (the AI pipeline is invisible)

### Onboarding Experience

The discovery form IS the onboarding, and it is well-designed as a step-by-step flow. But after submission, the user sees only "Thank you, [name]. I'll review your responses and come prepared for our conversation." They have no idea that four AI agents are about to process their submission through a Guardian -> Visionary -> Architect -> Oracle pipeline. This is a missed opportunity to demonstrate the technology itself.

**Option A:** Show a real-time progress screen after submission ("Your submission is being analyzed..." with stage indicators). This would be a powerful differentiator — no other consulting site does this.
**Option B:** Send a follow-up email with the assessment pipeline results — "Here's what our AI found about your business before we even talk."
**Option C:** Both.

### Error States, Empty States, Loading States

- Blog page with no published posts: unknown if there's an empty state
- Chat widget failure: falls back gracefully
- Discovery form API failure: falls back to mailto link (good)
- Contact form error: shows error message with email fallback (good)

### What's Missing That Nobody Asked About

- **Mobile experience quality:** The discovery form and chat widget need to work flawlessly on phones. Most B2B browsing starts on mobile.
- **Page speed / Core Web Vitals:** Heavy Framer Motion animations could impact LCP. This directly affects Google rankings.
- **Open Graph images and social sharing:** When someone shares askzev.ai on LinkedIn, what preview image appears? Custom OG images per page are important for social sharing in the consulting space.
- **Schema markup for services:** LocalBusiness or ProfessionalService schema for Google rich results. The blog has BlogPosting schema, but the services page likely lacks Service schema.
- **404 page:** Does one exist? Custom 404 with navigation back to key pages prevents dead-end bounces.

## 5. UX Patterns & User Journey Considerations

### The "I vs. We" Decision

This affects every page. Options with tradeoffs:

- **"I" throughout:** Most authentic for a solo practitioner. Research shows specialists who own personal brand convert better. Downside: some enterprise prospects may want to feel they're hiring a firm.
- **"We" throughout:** Current approach. Creates expectation of a team. Upside: feels bigger. Downside: prospect discovers it's one person and feels deceived.
- **Hybrid:** "I" on about page, "your team" or brand-neutral language elsewhere. Avoids the binary choice.
- **"Zev.AI" as brand, Zev as practitioner:** Separate the practice from the person. "Zev.AI builds... Zev leads every engagement personally."

### The Discovery Form: 12 Steps vs. Shorter Alternatives

Options with tradeoffs:

- **Keep 12 steps as the primary path:** Qualified leads only. Higher quality per submission, but lower volume.
- **Add a 3-field "quick contact":** Name, email, "what's on your mind?" alongside the full discovery. Captures lower-intent leads.
- **Add calendar booking:** "Skip the form, book a call" bypasses the form entirely. Highest conversion for ready-to-buy prospects.
- **Progressive disclosure:** Start with 3 questions, then optionally expand to the full 12. "Want a more tailored assessment? Answer a few more..."
- **Show the value proposition of the form more explicitly:** "Answer these questions and our AI will prepare a custom analysis before we talk — something other consultants charge $2,500 for."

### Homepage Social Proof Placement

Research says social proof should appear near CTAs. Current layout has case study summaries in section 4, but the hero CTA in section 1 and final CTA in section 6 have zero social proof adjacent.

**Pattern used by top-converting consulting sites:**
Hero -> 2-3 sentence proof strip ("Deployed 30+ AI agents across 5 industries") -> Logo bar -> Problem agitation -> Solution overview -> Case study highlight with metric + quote -> CTA with testimonial alongside.

## 6. Monetization & Business Model Considerations

### Pricing Strategy

Current pricing is transparent ("Starting from $2,500" for Assess, "$5,000" for Build, "$5,000/mo" for Optimize). This is relatively low for AI consulting and serves as a competitive advantage against firms charging $50k+ for assessments alone.

**Options:**
- **Keep transparent pricing:** Differentiator. Attracts budget-conscious SMBs. Filters out tire-kickers who can't afford even $2,500.
- **Remove pricing, replace with "request a quote":** Standard for higher-ticket consulting. Allows price customization per client. Risk: prospects assume "if you have to ask, you can't afford it."
- **Add pricing tiers with feature comparison table:** Visual pricing grid (like SaaS pricing pages) makes the value ladder clearer.
- **Anchor pricing:** Show a comparison to big-firm pricing ("Traditional consulting firms charge $50,000+ for what we deliver in the Assess phase for $2,500").

### Revenue Expansion Opportunities

- **Productized service offerings:** "AI Audit" as a fixed-price, fixed-scope product ($2,500 flat, delivered in 2 weeks, specific deliverable)
- **Retainer / fractional CTO model:** The Optimize tier could be positioned as "Fractional AI Officer" more prominently
- **Training / workshop offerings:** Half-day AI implementation workshops for teams
- **Templates / digital products:** AI implementation playbooks, prompt libraries, workflow templates as lower-ticket items
- **Referral program:** "Know someone who needs AI? Refer them and get a month free on your retainer"

## 7. Risks, Unknowns & Open Questions

1. **Are the case studies real and verifiable?** If a prospect Googles "ButcherBox AI" or contacts Blank Industries, will they confirm the engagement? If these are representative examples rather than real engagements, the wording "Representative examples of the outcomes we deliver" on the homepage could be a liability. It needs to be unambiguous.

2. **What is the actual conversion rate today?** Without analytics data, we're guessing at what's broken. How many visitors hit the homepage? How many reach /discover? How many complete the 12-step form? Where do they drop off? This data should drive priorities.

3. **How does the site perform on mobile?** Framer Motion animations, parallax scrolling, and multi-step forms can be problematic on mobile. B2B decision-makers often first encounter sites on their phones.

4. **Is the chat widget converting?** The auto-qualification feature (creating contacts and discoveries after 3+ messages) is clever, but is it actually firing? How many chats turn into pipeline entries?

5. **Does the blog have any published posts?** The content engine generates posts, but if the blog page is empty, it hurts credibility. A "Blog" nav link leading to an empty page is worse than no blog link at all.

6. **What is the SEO situation?** Is the site indexed? Ranking for any terms? Getting organic traffic? The AEO strategy (GPTBot, PerplexityBot allowed) is forward-thinking, but is it producing results?

7. **Is there a privacy policy page?** The discovery form collects personal data. The chat widget collects conversation data. GDPR/CCPA compliance requires a privacy policy.

8. **William Raveis dual positioning:** The about page prominently mentions William Raveis. Does this help (credibility, established career) or hurt (is this a side project?) the AI consulting positioning?

## 8. Things Nobody Asked About But Should Consider

### The "Proof of Technology" Paradox

The TOLA admin dashboard is genuinely impressive — 11 agents, real-time health monitoring, React Flow visualization, pipeline automation. But prospects never see any of it. The entire backend is behind /admin/login. The approach page describes the architecture abstractly, but showing is infinitely more powerful than telling.

**Possible solutions:**
- A read-only public dashboard view (anonymized data) that prospects can explore
- Screenshot gallery / video walkthrough of the admin as a portfolio piece
- A "/demo" page that embeds selected dashboard views
- Making the discovery form post-submission experience SHOW the pipeline working in real time

### The AI Chat Widget Is Burying the Lead

The chat widget sits as a small floating button. It auto-qualifies leads after 3+ messages and can auto-create discoveries. This is arguably the most innovative conversion mechanism on the site — and it's hidden in the bottom-right corner. Consider:
- Making the chat more prominent on specific pages
- Adding an inline chat experience on the homepage or services page
- Using the chat as a lightweight alternative to the 12-step form: "Tell our AI about your business and we'll prepare an assessment"

### Competitor Monitoring

The site is competing for attention with firms that have marketing teams. Ongoing needs:
- Monitoring what competitors rank for and creating content to compete
- Tracking whether AI search engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT) recommend zev.ai for relevant queries
- Building backlinks through guest posts, podcast appearances, or LinkedIn content

### The Presentation Page Concept

A "/for/[prospect-name]" or "/demo" page could be the site's most powerful conversion tool. What it could contain:
- Animated walkthrough of the TOLA architecture (simplified for non-technical audience)
- Side-by-side "before/after" showing manual process vs. AI-automated process
- Live metrics from a running system (anonymized)
- Interactive "build your own AI system" configurator (select your pain points, see recommended architecture)
- Embedded video of Zev explaining the approach (60-90 seconds)
- Direct "Book a call" calendar embed at the bottom
- Could be personalized per prospect if URL-parameterized

### Email Capture & Nurture Sequence

There is currently zero email marketing infrastructure beyond the contact/discovery forms. For a consulting business, email is typically the highest-ROI channel. Missing pieces:
- Newsletter signup form (footer, blog sidebar, dedicated page)
- Welcome email sequence (5-7 emails over 2-3 weeks: value content, case study, methodology, offer)
- Blog digest email (weekly/monthly roundup of new posts)
- Resend is already integrated for transactional email — extending to marketing email is straightforward

### The "One Person's Advantage" Angle

Instead of hiding the solo-practitioner reality, the site could lean into it as the primary differentiator: "When you hire a big firm, you get a junior associate. When you hire me, you get me. I designed the system, I built the system, I deployed the system. One person, full accountability, no layers of management between your problem and the solution." This messaging pattern is used by top-converting solo consultants across professional services.
